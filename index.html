My expertise includes data science, artificial intelligence (AI), probabilistic machine learning (ML), Bayesian inference, dynamical systems, computational neuroscience, and pharmacometrics analysis (PK/PD models), with applications to improve diagnostics, interventions, therapies and decision-making for brain-related medicine and digital health.


I have built causal virtual brain models (aka digital brain twin) for neurological disorders such as Epilepsy, Multiple Sclerosis (MS), Alcohol Use Disorder (AUC), Parkinson and Alzheimer's disease, as well as general anesthesia and healthy aging.

I have extensively worked with scientist, engineers and medical doctors in hospitals (e.g., La Timon Marseille) and industrial companies (e.g., SATT Sud-Est).
I have multiple patents (e.g., BVEP), and software development used in clinical trials (EPINOV; the first clinical trial in Epilepsy using virtual brain models, gathering data of 400 patients from 12 centres in France).

I have extensively used the softwares: TVB, Stan, PyMC3, NumPyro, scikit-learn, SBI.
I mainly program using Python, PyTorch, and TensorFlow.


I have extensive experience in parameter estimation by linking mathematical modeling, dynamical systems theory, and AI/ML algorithms, from a single neuron to the neural population (mean-fields) and whole-brain level , both analytically and numerically.

My current research focuses on Bayesian inference (adaptive HMC in probabilistic programming languages, and simulation-bassed inference using deep neural density estimators for flexible and efficient parameter estimation), with quantification of the associated uncertainty for decision making, from big data and high-dimensional nonlinear complex systems.
In particular, I use large-scale brain network models, also known as virtual brain models, which combine neural mass models with personalized anatomical data and probabilistic machine learning to fit neuroimaging data, such as (s)EEG/MEG/fMRI/LFP, with applications to epilepsy, general anesthesia, resting-state fMRI, brain aging, Parkinson, Alzheimer's, AUD, and other brain diseases.

My research goal is to build the theory and applications for causal virtual brain models by integrating various sources of information, such as neural mass models, anatomical data, empirical observations (neuroimaging recordings), advanced probabilistic machine learning, and principled statistical techniques, in a unified framework called the Bayesian Virtual Brain (BVB). This could provide reliable in-silico interventions such as closed-loop neurostimulator, digital drugs, as well as accurate and reliable predictions on brain trajectories in individuals suffering from brain diseases, particularly drug-resistant patients, for interventions, therapies, decision-making processes and clinical trials.
